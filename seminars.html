Construction of Public Buildings & Legal Processes

The Ministry of Environment and Urbanization has a significant role in the design and development of both the urban environments and the built environments. Their major function is to set standards law and regulations related to environmental issues, to create masterplans for urban projects, to control and participate in the process of repair and/or renovation projects, to determine the contract specifications, to create annual unit prices, consider and apply the climate change in all kinds of projects, and to determine general policies. Government’s involvement in projects may change according to the type and scale of project; they can be directly involved in the design of ministry buildings, embassy buildings, education buildings, health facilities, social facilities, parks…etc.

Environmentally Friendly "Green" Buildings & Certification Systems

Green building in architecture is the practice of designing and building structures that are sensitive and responsive to environmental conditions, meaning that they are environmentally responsible and resource-efficient throughout the building’s life span and life cycle which starts from its design process, continues with the construction, operation, maintenance, renovation/repair and even deconstruction phases. There are certification systems that controls and validates these green buildings, since built environment in general has an extensive direct and indirect impact on its environment. These certification systems include; green building standards, certifications and rating systems that aims at minimizing the impact of the built structure on the natural environment that it is situated in through the use of sustainable design approaches. Building Research Establishment’s Environmental Assembly Method (BREEAM), Green Building Council (USGBC), Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) are examples to some of these rating systems for new construction.

Project Management in the Construction Sector

This seminar will give an overview of construction management and introduce a new framework for re-organizing and using the “traditional” project management tool-kit. The framework is based on an interpretive analysis of 26 “stories" of project management in Turkey and around the world. The first author Selçuk Alten will illustrate the main concepts by chronicling these stories. The discussion will also include short histories (and critiques) of the sector in Turkey and of project management globally; the importance of design management and role of design thinking in project management; recommendations for legislation and rules governing the sector; and for project management as part of higher education.
